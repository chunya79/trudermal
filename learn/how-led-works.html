The Science

How Does Trudermal LED Technology Work?

A well-established, pain-free, and non-invasive treatment — LED light therapy stimulates your skin's natural healing process using targeted wavelengths of light.

The Basics

What Is LED Light Therapy?

LED (Light Emitting Diode) therapy uses specific wavelengths of light to trigger targeted cellular responses in skin and scalp tissue. Different wavelengths penetrate to different depths and produce different biological effects — from neutralising bacteria to stimulating collagen production to reactivating dormant hair follicles.

The treatment is completely non-invasive and pain-free. Unlike UV light, therapeutic LED wavelengths do not damage tissue — they support and accelerate the skin's natural repair and regeneration processes.

It's the same technology used in dermatology clinics and cosmetic medicine practices worldwide, now engineered by Trudermal for safe, effective home use.

LED can treat:

  • ✓ Active acne and breakouts
  • ✓ Fine lines and wrinkles
  • ✓ Uneven skin tone and texture
  • ✓ Redness and inflammation
  • ✓ Sun damage and dullness
  • ✓ Dermatitis and eczema
  • ✓ Hair thinning and follicle dormancy
  • ✓ Scalp inflammation and poor circulation

Cellular Biology

How LED Works at a Cellular Level

The key lies in the mitochondria — your cells' energy-producing powerhouses.

Modern lifestyle factors — chronic stress, poor sleep, UV exposure, pollution, and ageing — impair mitochondrial function. When mitochondria underperform, cells slow their communication and repair processes, leading to inflammation, acne, collagen breakdown, and the visible signs of ageing.

LED light energy is absorbed directly by mitochondria and restores cellular communication. Once "switched back on," skin cells begin:

  • → Producing more collagen and elastin
  • → Repairing damaged DNA
  • → Reducing inflammatory markers
  • → Increasing circulation and oxygenation
  • → Neutralising bacteria (acne)
  • → Reactivating dormant hair follicles

"LED is essential for the mitochondria — it turns the communication cell back on for improved function and cellular repair."

Consistency Is Key

LED therapy works cumulatively. Each session builds on the last. Most people begin seeing visible changes at 4–6 weeks of consistent use (3–4 sessions per week). For hair growth, allow 16 weeks for optimal results.

Results-Driven & Backed by Science

The Wavelengths

Blue · Red · Near-Infrared · Deep Near-Infrared

Each wavelength has a unique depth of penetration and biological target.

465nm
Blue Light

Targets Acne-Causing Bacteria

Blue light at 465nm penetrates the epidermis and is absorbed by porphyrins — compounds produced by P. acnes bacteria. This creates a toxic reaction within the bacteria, destroying them without harming surrounding tissue. Simultaneously, blue light reduces sebaceous (oil) gland activity to decrease pore congestion and future breakouts.

Anti-Acne Mode Acne Repair Mode
633nm
Red Light

Stimulates Collagen & Repairs Skin

Red light penetrates 2–3mm into the dermis, directly stimulating fibroblast cells to produce more collagen and elastin. This leads to firmer, plumper skin and a reduction in fine lines and wrinkles. Red light also increases local blood circulation, reduces inflammation and redness, and accelerates the skin's natural repair cycle.

Deep Cellular Repair & Anti-Inflammation

Near-infrared light penetrates deeper than visible wavelengths — reaching 5mm or more into tissue. At this depth it targets deeper collagen and elastin networks, supports cellular repair and regeneration, and has a powerful anti-inflammatory effect. It is particularly effective for scalp health and supporting the hair growth cycle.
